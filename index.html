A styling study for Metka Albreht

The everyday, elevated

It is the same woman, 35 years old, in both photographs. On the left she is wearing the kind of outfit she wore at 25. On the right, the same ordinary afternoon, dressed for who she is now. Nothing changes but the styling. That is the work.

1 · Jeans and a tee

The switch. Lose the rips and the logo, swap the skinny for a clean straight leg, good denim and a quality plain top. The same uniform, calmer and better cut.

2 · Leggings into town

The switch. Keep the comfort, drop the gym signals. A logo waistband and a cropped hoodie read borrowed from the gym. Soft tailoring in good fabric is just as easy and looks deliberate.

3 · The casual dress

The switch. Length and fabric. A clingy mini becomes a midi that skims, with a defined waist. The leg becomes a hint, not the headline.

4 · Cinema, then a drink

The switch. Fabric and cut instead of cut-outs and sparkle. A silk shirt does what the corset used to, and it carries you from the afternoon to the cinema to dinner with no costume change.

5 · Cold weather, coat-led

The switch. Make the coat the hero. A long wool coat over simple layers reads grown-up at once. A cropped puffer and lug boots read student. Same warmth, a different register.
